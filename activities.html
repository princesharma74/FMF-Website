REPORT OF FAKIR MOHAN FOUNDATION (FMF)’S ACTIVITIES IN THE LAST 5 YEARS

The foundation carries out its work under three categories or wings of FMF, viz Language & Culture, Socio-Economic Development and Education for Empowerment. The issue of language is uppermost on the FMF agenda.We have been conducting workshops in different prestigious institutions to this effect. The core concept for these are an interface of the socio-economic issues with language and literature. The interaction and participation of the student community features prominently in our workshop programme. The account of which can be found in the report given below. Most of our programs are to ‘YOUTH CENTRIC’ :

  • In the year 2015, a film screening on Fakir Mohan Senapati along with a discussion session titled ‘Odiya Identity and Fakir Mohan Senapati’ was held at India International Centre, New Delhi under the auspices of Odisha Forum New Delhi. Dr. J.P. Das, the eminent litterateur chaired the session and on the panel of speakers was Summary Satpathi, Professor Delhi University (Dept. of English) and Dr. Monica Das, Delhi University and Chairperson Fakir Mohan Foundation, New Delhi. Dr. Das spoke specifically on the making of the film Anwesan (Directed by her, and produced by Doordarshan Archives). This had won the National tele-film award for being the best docu-drama of the year.

  • OSA (Odisha Society of Americans) invited Dr. Monica Das to screen the film in Dallas, USA. 200 copies were sold by Doordarshan, Delhi to NROs in USA.

  • Dr. Monica Das represented FMF for delivering a lecture in OSA (Odisha Society for Americas) held in Seattle, USA on the topic of everyday use of mother tongue/ use of ‘Dhaga-Dhamali’ in the series of - Upholding our heritage and getting to our roots.

  • The film Anvesan was also screened in Dallas and Seattle, USA. On the request of Dr. Monica Das (chairperson), Fakir Mohan Foundation, 200 copies of the film were made and sold by Doordarshan to NRO’s. She was invited to speak on the making of the film in USA.

  • A press conference under the auspices of FMF was held in Bhubneswar Club, Odisha (pictures attached) on promoting the concept of Prachira Patra (wall magazine) in different schools of Odisha under the title “Bhasa Bahika”. This also included elements of “Manav Dharma”, a concept introduced by Monica Das as one of our mission statements. It is about learning humanism through language & literature. In the press meet eminent litterateurs and activists were present. They were Das Benhur, Prof GK Das, Haraprasad Das, Activist Biraja Das and Dr. Monica Das was also a part of it.
  • On 29.01.2016 a collaborative program along with Fakir Mohan University, Balasore was held in the University with the Vice Chancellor of the University presiding occasion. A three-day long function was held in Fakir Mohan University where scholars and experts on Fakir Mohan studies were invited from different parts of the country to speak on various aspects of Fakir Mohan. Dr. Monica Das also spoke at a session about the foundation and her association with the Senapati family. Incidentally Mohini Mohan Senapati is her grandfather.

  • A clipping of her film was shown. The CD Mohana Murchhana (based on the poems written by Fakir Mohan Senapati) was played to the benefit of the audience and especially students. FMF’s was extolled. On this occasion chairperson FMF Monica Das was felicitated by the dignitaries and ministers present on the day and citation was presented to her in appreciation of the leadership she has accorded to the foundation and her contribution as an educationist.

  • A heritage centre was opened within the premises of Fakir Mohan University in the year 2016 in the presence of Dr. Monica Das, Chairperson Fakir Mohan Foundation.

  • A three episode TV program with children from five different schools was presented on television in DD, BBSR. It was on the topic of FM’s literature-how much is being read by school children of Odisha – the extent of their exposure to this. It was an interactive three day session with leading litterateurs of Odisha including Pratibha Ray and Das Benhur. The recording is available with the foundation.

  • The Film titled “Anvesan” was made by Dr. Monica Das (Chair-person, Fakir Mohan Foundation (FMF)) on Fakir Mohan Senapati’s life & works. This was sponsored by Doordarshan. Later a DVD was made on it, sponsored by Doordarshan Archives & Prasar Bharti, Delhi. This was screened by the cultural department of government of Odisha

  • Fakir Mohan Foundation, New Delhi has launched a workshop program intended for the youth pan Odisha/ India titled ‘Back to Basics’ – a program for holistic development of the youth equipping them through soft skills for nation building. Workshop module design is in place - the unique element in the module is the interface of socio-economic concerns with language and literature. For this FMF has designed a module that has elements of humanism which are interfaced with literature and language of the great writers/thinkers of India. Humanism, it goes without saying, encompasses environmental consciousness, gender sensitization with special emphasis on gender equality, love for one’s heritage and realization about one’s roots/identity. We wish to carry out our agenda pan India. As a flagship movement we have already carried it out successfully in Odisha.

About Us

Fakir Mohan Foundation is a tribute to the heritage of Odisha with special focus on the legacy of Fakir Mohan Senapati the leading light of Indian literature. The foundation will work (globally, nationally and in Odisha), to deepen and sharpen people’s understanding of the rich socio- cultural roots and identity of its people.....more
