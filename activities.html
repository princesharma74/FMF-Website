REPORT OF FAKIR MOHAN FOUNDATION (FMF)’S ACTIVITIES IN THE LAST 5 YEARS

The foundation carries out its work under three categories or wings of FMF, viz Language & Culture, Socio-Economic Development and Education for Empowerment. The issue of language is uppermost on the FMF agenda.We have been conducting workshops in different prestigious institutions to this effect. The core concept for these are an interface of the socio-economic issues with language and literature. The interaction and participation of the student community features prominently in our workshop programme. The account of which can be found in the report given below. Most of our programs are to ‘YOUTH CENTRIC’ :

  • Chairperson Fakir Mohan Foundation, Monica Das was felicitated on the occasion of Fakir Mohan Senapati's 175th year of birthday celebration on 14th jan 2018 on Makar Sankranti at Jaydev Bhawan by Dr Sk Das from Das Foundation USA, Minister higher education Hon Ananta Das and minister finance Hon Shashi Bhusan Behera.

  • A souvenir of very highy quality was released on the occasion. Monica Das (Chairperson FMF) has contributed an article for this and is also on the editorial board of the magazine. Chief minister Naveen Patnaik was present on the occasion in the evening and had announced number of steps concerning the icon Fakir Mohan Senapati’s legacy to be popularized amongst youth in general. He announced that the steps should be put into practice with immediate effect along with a functioning website. This was indeed heartening news.

  • On the occasion of Odisha Art and lit Fest held on 2nd Feb, 2018. participating in the panel discussion were leading litterateurs of the land like Prof Himanshu Mahapatra, prof. Debendra Das, Prof. and vice and chancellor central univ, Prof. Sachidananda Mohanty (Moderator) , Prof. Rabindra Das from Visva Bharti University and Monica Das of FMF. This panel discussion was sponsored by a philanthropist and it was organized by Fakir Mohan Foundation, New Delhi. The panel discussion was titled 'Different facets of Fakir Mohan Senapati'. It was a three day festival with the participation of many foreign delegates of different nationalities.

  • Dr. Monica Das was invited to give the key-note address at Ravenshaw University in November, 2017 at a workshop that was held there. She was felicitated for taking forward the legacy of Fakir Mohan Senapati. She attended the workshop titled “Collision of Modernities in British Odisha: Early Social Realism in Fakir Mohan Senapati's Fictional Narratives”. Subsequently this book has been published by Amazon and it contains excerpts of Monica Das’s Memoirs relating to Fakir Mohan Senapati and the family.

  • Recently (Jan, 2018) a steering committee has been formed in Bhubaneswar for carrying out activities of the foundation on a regular basis. In this are associated Professors from five different universities in Odisha, activists, journalists and film makers. A state-wide essay competition, meant for students at all levels of education, based on Fakir Mohan Senapati’s life and works is to be conducted soon. There are many other programs on the agenda. This has had news coverage.

  • Fakir Mohan Foundation, New Delhi has launched a workshop program intended for the youth pan Odisha/ India titled ‘Back to Basics’ – a program for holistic development of the youth equipping them through soft skills for nation building. Workshop module design is in place - the unique element in the module is the interface of socio-economic concerns with language and literature. For this FMF has designed a module that has elements of humanism which are interfaced with literature and language of the great writers/thinkers of India. Humanism, it goes without saying, encompasses environmental consciousness, gender sensitization with special emphasis on gender equality, love for one’s heritage and realization about one’s roots/identity. We wish to carry out our agenda pan India. As a flagship movement we have already carried it out successfully in Odisha.

  • In the year 2015, a film screening on Fakir Mohan Senapati along with a discussion session titled ‘Odiya Identity and Fakir Mohan Senapati’ was held at India International Centre, New Delhi under the auspices of Odisha Forum New Delhi. Dr. J.P. Das, the eminent litterateur chaired the session and on the panel of speakers was Summary Satpathi, Professor Delhi University (Dept. of English) and Dr. Monica Das, Delhi University and Chairperson Fakir Mohan Foundation, New Delhi. Dr. Das spoke specifically on the making of the film Anwesan (Directed by her, and produced by Doordarshan Archives). This had won the National tele-film award for being the best docu-drama of the year.

  • A One Day Workshop on 'Gender Studies' n 'Green Studies' was organized by FM Foundation, New Delhi in collaboration with U N Autonomous College, Adashpur, Cuttack on 5th April 2018. Students and teachers participated in large numbers. There were experts from different universities who conducted the workshop, first of its kind, in a very adept manner. Sj Trilochan Kanungo, a veteran, nationalist and founder of this institution was full of energy and encouraged the students immensely. He bestowed a lot of credit to FM Foundation and particularly to Dr. Monica Das for the stewardship of this novel venture. Dr. Sita Kanta Das was felicitated on the occasion and Dr. Monica Das rendered the inaugural speech and presented the concept note of the workshop. Dr. Sudhakar Panda, vice chancellor Birla Global University, was the chief guest of the day.

About Us

Fakir Mohan Foundation is a tribute to the heritage of Odisha with special focus on the legacy of Fakir Mohan Senapati the leading light of Indian literature. The foundation will work (globally, nationally and in Odisha), to deepen and sharpen people’s understanding of the rich socio- cultural roots and identity of its people.....more
